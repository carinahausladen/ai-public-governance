# AI and Public Governance

**Core question:** How should governments, public institutions, and civil society govern, deploy, and respond to increasingly capable AI systems?

This is a politics/public administration course first, and an AI course second.

## Learning objectives

By the end, students should be able to:

1. Understand what modern AI systems can and cannot do.
2. Analyze public policy implications of AI.
3. Evaluate AI use in government and public services.
4. Assess risks, governance mechanisms, and regulatory approaches.
5. Design practical public-sector AI interventions.



---

## Module 2: AI and the State (Weeks 3–4)

Topics:

* Public-sector adoption
* Digital government
* Procurement
* Administrative capacity

Cases:

* Tax administration
* Benefits administration
* Immigration systems
* Public health


---

## Module 3: AI and Democracy (Weeks 5–6)

Topics:

* Elections
* Misinformation
* Political persuasion
* Civic participation

Cases:

* Election-related AI deployments
* Deepfakes
* Government communication

Exercise:
Students evaluate a hypothetical election commission's AI policy.

---

## Module 4: AI Policy and Regulation (Weeks 7–8)

Topics:

* Risk-based regulation
* The EU AI Act
* US approaches
* International governance

Key entities:

* OECD
* United Nations
* European Commission

Assignment:
Compare three governance frameworks.

---

## Module 5: AI for Public Good (Weeks 9–10)

This is where the OpenAI and Anthropic work becomes especially useful.

Topics:

* Education
* Healthcare
* Scientific discovery
* Public services
* Nonprofit applications

Students track:

* OpenAI education initiatives
* Anthropic education initiatives
* Public-benefit programs
* Economic impact research


---

## Module 6: Future Governance (Weeks 11–12)

Topics:

* Frontier AI governance
* National AI strategies
* Compute governance
* Institutional design

Final question:

> What institutions should exist in 2035 that do not exist today?

# weekly activity

Have students maintain a **"Frontier AI Governance Tracker."**

Every week they monitor:

* [OpenAI Newsroom](https://openai.com/news/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
* [Anthropic News](https://www.anthropic.com/news?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
* [OECD AI Observatory](https://oecd.ai?utm_source=chatgpt.com)
* [Stanford HAI](https://hai.stanford.edu?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

Each student submits:

* one development,
* one governance implication,
* one policy recommendation.

This keeps the course current even as the technology changes.
