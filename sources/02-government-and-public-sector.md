## OpenAI

### Introducing ChatGPT Gov
Source: https://openai.com/global-affairs/introducing-chatgpt-gov/ *(reconstructed via search — verify)*

A tailored version of ChatGPT giving U.S. government agencies an additional way to access OpenAI's frontier models.

- **Deployment & security:** Agencies can deploy ChatGPT Gov in their own Microsoft Azure commercial cloud or Azure Government cloud (on Azure OpenAI Service), letting them manage their own security/privacy/compliance — including stringent frameworks such as **IL5, CJIS, ITAR, and FedRAMP High**.
- **Features:** Many ChatGPT Enterprise capabilities — saving/sharing conversations within a government workspace, uploading text and image files, access to GPT-4o (text interpretation, summarization, coding, image analysis, mathematical reasoning), and the ability to create/share custom GPTs for agency-specific needs.
- **Related:** ChatGPT Enterprise to be made available to the entire federal executive-branch workforce at essentially no cost via the GSA partnership ($1 per agency for one year — see below).

### Introducing OpenAI for Government
Source: https://openai.com/global-affairs/introducing-openai-for-government/ *(reconstructed via search — verify)*

An umbrella consolidating OpenAI's government efforts — previously announced customers/partnerships plus ChatGPT Gov — under one program.

- Brings existing collaborations with **U.S. National Labs, the Air Force Research Laboratory, NASA, NIH, and the Treasury** under one roof.
- Anchored by the **GSA OneGov partnership** making ChatGPT Enterprise available to the entire federal executive branch at ~no cost for a year ($1/agency), plus a 60-day window of unlimited advanced models/features (Deep Research, Advanced Voice Mode).
- Deployment partners **Slalom** and **Boston Consulting Group** support secure, responsible rollout and training.
- (Secondary reporting, 2026) OpenAI later expanded its government footprint via an **AWS** deal to sell products to the U.S. government for classified and unclassified work, following a **Pentagon** deal allowing military use of its models on a classified network.

### Solutions for government
Source: https://openai.com/solutions/industries/government/ *(reconstructed via search — verify)*

- **Scale:** Since 2024, more than **90,000 users across 3,500+ U.S. federal, state, and local agencies** have sent **18M+ messages** on ChatGPT for daily work.
- **Offering:** All levels of government get access to ChatGPT Enterprise and ChatGPT Gov in "secure and compliant environments," plus hands-on support.
- **Use cases:**
  - **Air Force Research Laboratory** — ChatGPT Enterprise for admin use cases, internal-resource access, basic coding, AI education.
  - **Los Alamos National Laboratory** — scientific research and innovation.
  - **State of Minnesota** (Enterprise Translations Office) — faster, more accurate translation for multilingual communities via ChatGPT Team; reduced cost and turnaround.
  - **Commonwealth of Pennsylvania** — first-in-the-nation AI pilot; participants saved ~105 minutes/day on routine tasks on days used.
- **Strategic framing:** potential to help the public sector tackle complex challenges — public health, infrastructure, national security.

### GSA Announces New Partnership with OpenAI, Delivering Deep Discount to ChatGPT Gov-Wide Through MAS
Source: https://www.gsa.gov/about-gsa/newsroom/news-releases/gsa-announces-new-partnership-with-openai-delivering-deep-discount-to-chatgpt-08062025
Date: August 6, 2025. *(scraped directly)*

GSA and OpenAI announced a partnership making **ChatGPT Enterprise** available to federal agencies at substantially reduced rates, aligned with the White House AI modernization efforts.

- Participating agencies get ChatGPT Enterprise for a **nominal $1/year**, plus a **60-day period of unlimited advanced model usage** at no cost.
- Includes a dedicated government user community and customized training resources; partner-led learning sessions.
- Supports two OMB memoranda on accelerating federal AI adoption and streamlining acquisition.
- Available through GSA's **Multiple Award Schedule (MAS)**.

Quotes:
> "Our government's effective use of AI is critical to demonstrating we are the world's AI leader." — Michael Rigas, GSA Acting Administrator
> "We're proud to partner with the General Services Administration, delivering on President Trump's AI Action Plan." — Sam Altman, OpenAI CEO
> "GSA is making it easier for federal agencies to access powerful AI tools—improving productivity and efficiency." — Josh Gruenbaum, Federal Acquisition Service Commissioner

## Cross-lab observations (for course design)

- OpenAI's public-sector strategy is built around **near-free access at scale** (the $1/agency GSA OneGov deal) plus a **compliance story** (FedRAMP High, IL5, CJIS, ITAR via Azure Government).
- Strong emphasis on **named agency case studies** with quantified efficiency gains (Pennsylvania's ~105 min/day).
- A clear **policy alignment frame** (White House AI Action Plan, OMB memos) — useful for a public-governance course discussing the politics of procurement.
- Gap to fill for symmetry: Anthropic's own government offering, to compare safety-first positioning vs. scale-first positioning.
