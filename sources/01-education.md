### Video — Building an AI-Powered University (2025)
Source: https://forum.openai.com/public/videos/building-an-ai-powered-university-2025
Posted March 12, 2025 · ~3.1K views · Tags: Higher Education, AI Adoption, AI Literacy · Moderator: Natalie Cone (OpenAI Forum Community Architect).

**Speakers:** Leah Belsky (VP & GM, Education @ OpenAI; ex-CRO Coursera); Siya Raj Purohit (Education GTM @ OpenAI); Jason Horne (Partner @ GSV Ventures); Dr. Jaci Jenkins Lindburg (Assoc. Vice Chancellor @ University of Nebraska Omaha); Axel Persaud (AVP Enterprise Engineering @ University of Maryland); David Brubaker (Senior IT Director for AI Initiatives @ The Wharton School).

**Three institutional approaches to AI adoption:**
- **University of Nebraska at Omaha** — Deployed 1,000 ChatGPT EDU licenses via a semester-based RFP process targeting faculty innovation. 150 initial proposals; high renewal rates.
- **University of Maryland** — Universal campus access via consumption-based model with tiered quotas. Built a virtual study assistant and a Workday transition assistant chatbot (~700 questions/day at launch; 10,000+ questions over two weeks).
- **Wharton School** — Full access for MBA students, faculty, staff with targeted pilots. ~50% active MBA student adoption; privacy concerns and lack of perceived differentiation cited as barriers.

**Key themes:** operational efficiency (staff reported 2–10+ hrs/week saved); teaching & learning (academic integrity vs. innovation; "lecture recall" pilots letting students search class transcripts to video timestamps); implementation strategy (avoid big custom builds, start with power users, communicate, partner with vendors); AI literacy & workforce development (Cengage survey: ~2-in-3 employers expect AI competency; >50% of students feel unprepared).

**Panelist recommendations:** start small; communicate strategically (opportunity, not disruption); solve real problems first; cross-functional collaboration; leverage external resources; assume rapid change (design for flexibility).

## Anthropic

### Introducing Claude for Education
Source: https://www.anthropic.com/news/introducing-claude-for-education
Date: April 2, 2025.

A specialized version of Claude for higher education, enabling universities to develop AI-enabled approaches across teaching, learning, and administration. Four major components:

1. **Learning Mode** — Guides student reasoning through Socratic questioning rather than giving direct answers (e.g., "How would you approach this problem?"); operates within Projects; provides structured templates for research papers and study guides.
2. **University-Wide Access** — Full campus agreements with Northeastern University, the London School of Economics (LSE), and Champlain College.
3. **Academic Partnerships** — With Internet2 and Instructure to integrate AI into the Canvas LMS.
4. **Student Programs** — Claude Campus Ambassadors program and API credits for student-led projects.

**Use cases:** Students — literature reviews with citations, step-by-step calculus, thesis feedback. Faculty — outcome-aligned rubrics, essay feedback, problem generation. Admin staff — enrollment-trend analysis, email automation, converting policy docs to FAQs.

**Campus partners:** Northeastern (first university design partner; 50,000 students/faculty/staff across 13 global campuses; President Joseph E. Aoun, author of *Robot-Proof*). LSE (equitable access; President Larry Kramer: "As social scientists, we are in a unique position to understand and shape how AI can positively transform education and society."). Champlain College (workplace AI competencies; President Alex Hernandez: "AI is changing what it means to be Ready for Work.").

### Anthropic Education Report: How Educators Use Claude
Source: https://www.anthropic.com/news/anthropic-education-report-how-educators-use-claude
Published: August 27, 2025.

Analysis of ~74,000 anonymized higher-ed conversations on Claude.ai (May–June 2025), plus qualitative research with 22 Northeastern University faculty.

**Findings:**
- Curriculum development = 57% of analyzed conversations; academic research = 13%; student assessment = 7%. (Gallup: AI saves teachers ~5.9 hrs/week.)
- Faculty use Claude Artifacts to build interactive tools (chemistry simulations, grading rubrics, dashboards, educational games).
- Selective automation: student-facing/creative tasks (lesson design, advising, grant writing) lean augmentation (67–77%); routine admin (e.g., financial management) leans automation (65%).
- Grading is contentious: 7% of conversations, but 48.9% automation-heavy — yet faculty rated it AI's least effective use case.
- Pedagogy reshaping: one professor — "I will never again assign a traditional research paper."
- **Limitations:** captured ~1.5% of higher-ed email conversations; excluded K-12; early-adopter bias; Claude.ai-only.

## Cross-lab observations (for course design)

- Both labs anchor on **higher education** first, with named flagship university partners (OpenAI: Wharton, Harvard, Maryland, Nebraska-Omaha; Anthropic: Northeastern, LSE, Champlain).
- Both pair product (ChatGPT EDU / Claude for Education) with **advisory/community structures** (OpenAI Forum group; Anthropic Higher Education Advisory Board) and **AI-literacy curricula** (AI Fluency courses).
- Anthropic emphasizes a **pedagogy stance** (Learning Mode, Socratic questioning) and publishes **usage-data research**; OpenAI emphasizes **institutional rollout playbooks** and operational efficiency case studies.
- Shared tension throughout: **academic integrity / "voice"** vs. productivity and adoption.
