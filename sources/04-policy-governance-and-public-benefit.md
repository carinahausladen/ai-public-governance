## Anthropic

### Company Overview
Source: https://www.anthropic.com/company

Anthropic describes itself as "an AI safety and research company" building "reliable, interpretable, and steerable AI systems."

**Three pillars:** Safety Research (AI safety as "a systematic science"); Interdisciplinary approach (researchers, engineers, policy experts, business leaders); Industry-wide collaboration (AI companies as "one piece of a big puzzle," partnering with civil society, government, academia, nonprofits, industry).

**Seven core values:**
1. Global Good — decisions that "maximize positive outcomes for humanity in the long run"
2. Balance — acknowledging risks and benefits
3. User Care — treating customers, policymakers, employees, and affected communities as "users"
4. Safety Leadership — inspiring "a race to the top" on safety
5. Pragmatism — "the simplest solution" that works
6. Integrity — "a high-trust, low-ego organization"
7. Mission Focus — the mission as "the final arbiter in our decisions"

**Governance:** Operates as a **Public Benefit Corporation**. Board: Dario Amodei, Daniela Amodei, Yasmin Razavi, Reed Hastings, Chris Liddell, Vas Narasimhan. A **Long-Term Benefit Trust** (Neil Buddy Shah, Richard Fontaine, Mariano-Florentino Cuéllar) provides oversight.

### The Long-Term Benefit Trust (LTBT)
Source: https://www.anthropic.com/news/the-long-term-benefit-trust
Date: September 19, 2023.

An independent governance body to address challenges of transformative AI. Five financially disinterested members with authority to select and remove board members, eventually holding a majority.

- Operates alongside the PBC structure; ensures the company "responsibly balances the financial interests of stockholders with the interests of those affected by Anthropic's conduct."
- Rationale: AI creates "unprecedentedly large externalities" (from national-security risks to safety benefits); traditional corporate structures don't account for impacts on non-contracting parties (the general public).
- **Mechanics:** Class T stock held exclusively by the Trust grants voting authority; board representation phases in by time/funding milestones; the Trust achieves board majority within ~4 years.
- **Initial trustees:** Jason Matheny (RAND), Kanika Bahl (Evidence Action), Neil Buddy Shah (Clinton Health Access Initiative, Chair), Paul Christiano (Alignment Research Center), Zach Robinson (Effective Ventures US). *(Page footnote notes several trustee changes through January 2026.)*

### Introducing The Anthropic Institute
Source: https://www.anthropic.com/news/the-anthropic-institute
Date: Mar 11, 2026.

A new entity to address challenges posed by powerful AI and share research with researchers and the public during the transition to advanced AI.

- **Context:** within five years models advanced from initial commercial releases to systems that can discover cybersecurity vulnerabilities and handle diverse real-world tasks; leadership predicts "far more dramatic progress will follow in the next two years."
- **Core questions:** how powerful AI reshapes employment/economies; societal-resilience opportunities; emerging threats; values embedded in AI systems; governance frameworks for recursive self-improving systems.
- **Structure:** Led by co-founder **Jack Clark as Head of Public Benefit**. Integrates three existing teams — Frontier Red Team (capability stress-testing), Societal Impacts (real-world usage), Economic Research (labor/economic effects) — and adds new teams on AI forecasting and legal-system interactions.
- **Leadership appointments:** Matt Botvinick (AI and rule of law); Anton Korinek (economic research on transformative AI); Zoë Hitzig (connecting economic research to model development).
- **Policy expansion:** expanded Public Policy org under Sarah Heck; new DC office opening in spring; expanded global presence.

### Focus areas for The Anthropic Institute (research agenda)
Source: https://www.anthropic.com/research/anthropic-institute-agenda
Date: May 7, 2026.

The Anthropic Institute (TAI) studies AI's societal impact from within a frontier lab and publishes findings for organizations and governments. **Four research focus areas:**

1. **Economic Diffusion** — how AI deployment transforms economies (adoption patterns, productivity growth, labor impacts, future of work; whether AI is a "general purpose technology"; distributing economic gains broadly).
2. **Threats and Resilience** — dual-use capabilities, societal resilience, offense-defense dynamics, crisis-planning infrastructure, surveillance implications.
3. **AI Systems in the Wild** — how people/institutions interact with AI (epistemological shifts, critical-thinking degradation, interface design, governance of autonomous agents).
4. **AI-Driven R&D** — how AI accelerates research (including AI building successor AI), governance mechanisms, acceleration control points, distribution of scientific progress.

**Initiatives:** sharing granular Economic Index data; frontier red-team research; Anthropic Fellowship opportunities for external researchers.

## Cross-lab observations (for course design)

- Anthropic offers a rich set of **formal governance artifacts** — PBC status, the Long-Term Benefit Trust (with real board-control mechanics via Class T stock), and now The Anthropic Institute with a named **Head of Public Benefit**.
- Strong throughline: governing **externalities** that markets and ordinary corporate law miss — directly relevant to a public-governance course.
- The Institute's agenda **bridges** this topic with **Education** (AI literacy, critical thinking), **Government** (policy frameworks), and **Societal & Economic Impacts** (economic diffusion).
- Key compare/contrast for the course: Anthropic's **trust-based control** vs. OpenAI's **foundation/PBC** model — two different answers to "who governs a frontier lab for the public good."
