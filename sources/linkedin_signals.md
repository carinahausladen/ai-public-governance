# LinkedIn Signals Scan

Compiled on 2026-05-29.

This file captures useful LinkedIn signals across the course domains. It is intentionally selective. Most LinkedIn content on AI governance is promotional, repetitive, or vendor-driven. The goal here is to identify the posts, people, and organizations that are analytically useful for teaching.

Use this file as:

- a source of **platform discourse** for the syllabus;
- a way to identify **people and organizations to monitor** during the term;
- evidence of how AI governance is translated into managerial, procurement, education, and public-sector language.

Important caution:

- LinkedIn is best treated as a record of **framing, positioning, hiring, and agenda-setting**, not as a neutral factual source.
- The most useful signals are often not the "best" posts, but the ones that reveal how actors want AI governance to be understood.

## 5. Best uses in the course

- Use LinkedIn as a **platform discourse layer** alongside lab documents and journalism.
- Use it especially for:
  - procurement and implementation;
  - compliance and managerial language;
  - education adoption narratives;
  - Switzerland as a live governance case;
  - staffing and institutional build-out.
- Do **not** rely on LinkedIn as the main source for contested facts when official or journalistic sources exist.

---

## 6. Bottom line

The strongest LinkedIn signal across these domains is not any single viral post. It is a repeated pattern:

- companies launch products, partnerships, and public-sector offerings first;
- LinkedIn translates these moves into managerial language about readiness, compliance, and adoption;
- public institutions respond through procurement, staffing, guidance, and coordination.

That pattern fits the central premise of the course very well.
